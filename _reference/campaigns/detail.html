About This Project

Clean water should not be a luxury. Yet over 2 billion people worldwide lack access to safely managed drinking water, and the systems available to them are either prohibitively expensive, impossible to repair locally, or locked behind proprietary designs that prevent communities from adapting the technology to their specific conditions.

The Open Source Water Purifier is a community-designed, field-repairable water purification system built for off-grid communities, disaster relief operations, and rural households. It combines a ceramic filter element for sediment and bacteria removal with a UV-C LED array for viral disinfection, all powered by a small solar panel and battery pack that can operate independently of any power grid.

Every component has been chosen for global availability. The housing is made from bamboo composite instead of injection-molded plastic, meaning it can be fabricated in any workshop with basic woodworking tools. The electronics use through-hole components wherever possible, so the control board can be hand-soldered without specialized reflow equipment. And the ceramic filter elements are manufactured using locally sourced clay and colloidal silver, following a process documented by Potters for Peace and adapted for small-batch production.

This is not a proof of concept. We have been field testing Prototype v3 in three villages in Karnataka since January 2026, processing an average of 120 liters per day per unit. The results from independent lab testing confirm removal of 99.97% of bacteria and 99.9% of viruses in source water samples taken from local bore wells and surface water.

What's Included

  • Fully assembled water purifier unit with bamboo composite housing
  • Ceramic filter element (replaceable, rated for 6 months of continuous use)
  • UV-C LED array module with driver circuit
  • 10W solar panel with mounting bracket
  • 12V 6Ah LiFePO4 battery pack with charge controller
  • Flow sensor and basic water quality indicator (TDS)
  • Complete assembly guide and maintenance manual (printed)
  • Access to all source files: KiCad schematics, STEP models, firmware source, and BOM spreadsheet
