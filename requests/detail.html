Open Source Desktop CNC Machine

High Demand Tools & Equipment

Submitted by Ravi Menon · 3 months ago · Last edited 2 days ago

Description

There is a glaring gap in the market for a truly open-source desktop CNC machine that a hobbyist or small makerspace can afford, build, and maintain without depending on any single vendor. We need a compact router capable of PCB milling (isolation routing down to 0.1mm trace/space), wood engraving, and light aluminum work — all in a footprint that fits on a workbench.

Existing options fall short in critical ways. The Shapeoko is excellent hardware but closed-source and expensive. The MPCNC (Mostly Printed CNC) is admirably open but relies on 3D-printed structural parts that flex under load, limiting precision for PCB work. The PrintNC is a steel-frame beast — powerful but oversized and over-budget for most hobbyists. None of these ship with open electronics: you're always bolting on a proprietary controller board that can't be audited, modified, or locally manufactured.

What the community needs is a machine designed from the ground up to be fully open: mechanical CAD in FreeCAD, electronics in KiCad, firmware forked from Grbl-HAL, and a complete BOM sourced from globally available parts. The target is under $500 for a complete kit, buildable in a weekend, and precise enough to mill a double-sided PCB. Everything published under CERN-OHL-S so anyone can manufacture, modify, and sell improvements. No black boxes. No vendor lock-in. No planned obsolescence.
